## 🖥️ Using the interface

The main window shows your active projects. Each project acts as a container for your production work.

### Create a new project

1. Click the New Project button in the top menu.
2. Enter a name for your production.
3. Choose a folder where the software stores your work.
4. Click Save.

### Adding scenes

You define the flow of your film through the scene manager.

1. Open your project.
2. Click the Add Scene button.
3. Give your scene a descriptive title.
4. Input the date of the shoot and the location.
5. Assign specific actors to the scene.

The software updates your schedule automatically after you save each scene. You view your full production timeline by clicking the Schedule tab.

## 🛠️ Managing actors

Digital production needs a list of participants. The actor database helps you find contact information and casting notes.

1. Click the Actors tab in the side menu.
2. Select Add New Person.
3. Type the legal name and contact details for the actor.
4. Upload a headshot if you have one available.
5. Link the actor to one or more scenes in your project.

## 💾 Saving your work

The software saves your changes every few minutes. You click the Save icon in the top left corner to force a manual save. Do not shut down your computer while a save operation occurs. This ensures your project data remains intact.

## 📋 Best habits for directors

Organize your files by project name. Use dates in your file names to track progress. Back up your project folder to an external hard drive or a cloud service once a week. This protects your work from hardware failure.

No. DirectorSkill functions without an active internet connection. You stay productive even on remote film sets.

Can I move my project to another computer?
Yes. Copy your project folder to a USB drive and move it to another machine. Open the project folder through the software menu on the new device.

Does the software support large projects?
Yes. DirectorSkill handles hundreds of scenes without loss of speed. Ensure your computer meets the memory requirements for large projects.

What happens if the software stops responding?
Close the application and reopen it. If the issue continues, restart your computer. The auto-save function keeps your last session data safe.
